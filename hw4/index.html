Part 2

  • With a low ks, the clothbounces and stretches a lot more. With a high ks, the cloth doesn’t bend much, and acts more like a plane.
  • With a high density, parts of the cloth closer to the pinned points fall quickly and then bounce up causing a ripple. When the density is low the cloth acts similarly to a low ks, which is not bending much.
  • A low damping causes the cloth to swing back and forth a lot more. A high damping causes it to appear almost in slow motion, as it slowly falls without retaining inertia.

A low damping causes the cloth to bounce back.

Low density and high ks causes the cloth to look close to a square.

High density and low ks causes the top to sag more
